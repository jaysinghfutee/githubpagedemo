Conway's Law Organizations design systems that mirror their own internal communication structures. — Melvin Conway, 1967
📖 The Mythical Man-Month · Key Lessons Frederick P. Brooks Jr., 1975
01
Adding people slows things down
Adding engineers to a late project makes it later. New members need onboarding time and increase communication overhead exponentially.
02
Communication overhead grows quadratically
As team size grows, the number of communication paths explodes. 4 people = 6 paths. 8 people = 28 paths. Structure and tooling must absorb this.
03
Plan to throw one away
The first system is never right. Build expecting to iterate. Jira gives us visibility to learn fast and adapt — without losing context between versions.
04
Conceptual integrity over committee design
A system with one clear owner is better than one designed by many voices. One integration hub — Jira — is the single source of truth for all coordination.

The Surgical Team Model
Brooks' ideal small team structure

Brooks argued the most effective team is like a surgical unit — one lead surgeon makes all key decisions, supported by specialists who amplify their output without creating noise. Small, focused, fast.

🎯 Lead — owns decisions ⚙️ Support — removes blockers 📝 Documenter — preserves context 🔍 QA — enforces quality
⚡
The One Exception to Communication Overhead
When tasks are so clear they need no coordination

Brooks noted that when a task is so well-defined that it requires no back-and-forth, parallelisation works and overhead vanishes. The goal of any system is to manufacture this clarity at scale.

How Slack + Jira + Confluence build this clarity
📋
Jira makes every task explicit — assignee, scope, acceptance criteria. No ambiguity about who does what or when it's done.
📚
Confluence stores the why behind every decision. Anyone gets full context without asking — async clarity at scale.
💬
Slack handles residual coordination — fast, channel-structured, linked back to Jira so nothing gets lost in a thread.

Before — How We Were Actually Working
The real cost of fragmented communication with OMBU & EGW
OMBU
via Email
✉️
4–6 hour reply times
Updating a single Python dependency — something that takes 2 minutes to action — would spiral into 3 to 4 back-and-forth emails before anyone agreed on the version or approach.
📅
Charged meetings just to align
4 to 6 meetings per month billed back to us, just to get on the same page on tasks that could have been a single Jira ticket with acceptance criteria.
🔍
No task linkage or audit trail
Decisions made in email threads had no home. Context got buried. New people joining had no way to understand why a dependency was pinned to a specific version.
Example — updating a Python dependency
Hey, can you bump requests to 2.31.0 in requirements.txt? There's a security fix.
Sure — is this for the main service or the data pipeline?
Main service. Both actually — everywhere it's used.
Got it. Do you want us to run the test suite after or just open a PR?
Open a PR, run tests, let me know results. Please do it this sprint.
Which branch should we target?
⏱ 2–3 days · 6 emails · PR not yet opened
EGW
via Google Meet + Teams (mostly email)
🔐
Login friction every call
EGW used Google Meet. We had to log into a shared Google account every time to join a call. Different to our Teams setup — awkward, slow, easy to miss meetings.
📨
Hard to reach — defaulted to email
Teams calls rarely connected. Email became the fallback — which meant the same 4–6 hour lag, thread fragmentation and no way to track what was agreed on a dependency change.
🌀
No single place for updates
Status updates lived across email, Teams chat and Google Meet notes — or in someone's head. No visibility without chasing. No record of why a version was chosen.

Team Topologies
Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais · 2019 · How to structure teams for fast flow
Stream-aligned
🌊
Product / Feature Team
The primary team type. Aligned to a flow of work — a product, service, or user journey. Owns their domain end to end. At Futee, this is the core build team.
owns outcomes
Enabling
🔧
DevOps / Platform Team
Helps stream teams unblock themselves. Brings capability, not dependency. Temporary by design — they up-skill and step back.
reduces cognitive load
Complicated Subsystem
🧮
Specialist Domain Team
Handles deep expertise areas — compliance, security, ML. Called in when the cognitive load is too high for a stream team alone. Vanta lives here.
deep expertise
Platform
🏗️
Internal Platform
Provides self-service infrastructure to all other teams. AWS + Jira + Confluence + Slack together form Futee's internal platform layer.
self-service foundation
Three Interaction Modes
🤝
Collaboration — two teams work closely together for a defined period to discover new things. Then they separate. Not a permanent state.
📡
X-as-a-Service — one team consumes something another provides with minimal interaction. AWS to Futee is this. Clean, low-friction.
🎓
Facilitating — an enabling team helps a stream team level up, then steps away. Avoids permanent dependency and bottlenecks.
⚡ How this maps to Futee right now
Futee core team is the stream-aligned team — owns delivery end to end
AWS + Jira + Slack + Confluence = the platform layer — self-service, low friction
Vanta = complicated subsystem — deep compliance expertise feeding into Jira
EGW & OMBU onboarded via Slack = moving toward X-as-a-Service interaction mode

The 4 DORA Metrics — What elite teams measure
📦
Deployment Frequency
How often you ship to production. Elite: multiple times per day. Jira tracks every release cycle — giving visibility across the team.
deploy often
⏱️
Lead Time for Changes
Time from commit to production. Elite: under one hour. GitHub linked to Jira means every PR has a ticket — full traceability of cycle time.
ship fast
🔥
Change Failure Rate
% of deployments causing incidents. Elite: 0–15%. Vanta → Jira ensures compliance issues surface as tasks before they become failures.
fail less
🔁
Mean Time to Recovery
How fast you recover when things break. Elite: under one hour. AWS CloudWatch → Jira → Slack means the right people are notified in seconds.
recover fast
Key Capabilities Accelerate Identifies · Applied to Futee
🔀 Trunk-based development
Small, frequent commits to a shared branch. GitHub + Jira branch linking enforces discipline — every branch traces to a ticket.
📋 Work visibility
Accelerate found that teams with clear work-in-progress limits outperform those without. Jira is our WIP board — limits context switching.
🔄 Loosely coupled architecture
Teams that can deploy independently move faster. Our tool stack is designed so each integration (Slack, AWS, Vanta) fires into Jira independently.
📣 Generative culture
Accelerate links culture to performance. High-trust, high-information flow. Slack as the communication layer surfaces information without gatekeeping.
📚 Documentation as code
Decisions are as important as code. Confluence linked to Jira issues means every architectural decision has a home and a trail.
⚡ Fast feedback loops
Elite teams get signal fast and act. AWS → Jira → Slack closes the loop from alarm to ticket to notification in under a minute.
